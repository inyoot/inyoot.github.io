Plutarch says that Sertorius, in order to teach his soldiers that perseverance and wit are better that brute force, had two horses brought before them, and set two men to pull out their tails.

One of the men was a burly Hercules, who tugged and tugged, but all to no purpose.

The other was a sharp, weasel-faced tailor, who plucked one hair at a time, amidst roars of laughter, and soon left the tail quite bare.Â
